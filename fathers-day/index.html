5 reasons why this makes the perfect gift for Father's Day

(and also a tutorial on how to get your dad to grill you more BBQ)

By Marlene Hart | 3 min read

I'm not going to lie. This is the first Father's Day gift i've given my dad in five years that he actually uses every weekend.

Here are the 5 reasons it worked.

01

It solves the worst part of grilling.

If you didn't know, cleaning the grill is the worst part of grilling. You know that feeling of scrubbing a burnt baking sheet? Or staring at the bottom of the pasta pot after marinara sat in it overnight?

That's basically what a dirty grill is. Except it's outside. It's summer. It's hot. You just cooked for everyone. And now you're supposed to stand there and scrub for 30 minutes.

This is electric. You just run it across the grates, and it's done in like 60 seconds. It's the same energy as the first time you used a dishwasher and realized you'd been hand-washing for no reason.

Trust me. If your dad likes grilling, he will be SO into this.

02

Dads will not buy this for themselves. Ever.

I don't know what it is about dads. They will use the same $5 wire brush from Home Depot for ten years. They refuse to upgrade. It's just a thing they do.

Which is the whole reason this works as a Father's Day gift. You're giving him the upgrade he'd never get for himself.

Bonus: every guy who comes over is going to want to try it. You've basically made him the cool dad on the block.

03

He'll send you pictures. That's the whole thing.

My dad is not an emotional guy. He doesn't have social media, he barely answers his texts, and he literally never smiles in family photos. He's a quiet, "I don't need anything" kind of dad.

I gave him this on Father's Day. By the next weekend, he was sending me pictures of himself grilling with his buddies. And smiling in them.

I'm not going to lie, I started crying a little bit at work.

If your dad isn't big on showing feelings, this is the gift that pulls something out of him. And yes — you'll have the screenshots.

04

You'll get invited to more Sunday BBQs. (It's selfish, in a good way.)

Nobody tells you this part about giving your dad a really good gift: he ends up using it more, so he grills more, which means YOU get invited to more Sundays.

Ribs. Pulled pork. Brisket. The works.

My mom told me he asked her last week — "is Marlene coming Sunday?"

Honestly, I didn't realize I was buying a seat back at the table. But I was.

05

And if he doesn't love it, you can send it back.

30-day money-back guarantee. 1-year warranty. If your dad somehow doesn't love it, you ship it right back. No drama.

But trust me — he's going to love it. Dads who grill end up obsessed with this thing.
